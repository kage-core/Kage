Goal: line up 20–30 short-form creators in the AI-coding niche to post authentic
"here's a tool I use" videos, each ending in **try it / book a demo**. This file
is the sourcing kit — I can't hire or pay creators (that's your spend + your
accounts), but everything here makes it a copy-paste afternoon.

> Reality check: this is a **paid** channel (~$15–30/video + performance bonus).
> Only worth it once the product converts — point creators at the free
> `kage scan` hook and the demo booking, not a cold install.

## Who to target (creator ICP)

Small-to-mid creators (5k–100k followers) whose audience is **developers using AI
coding tools** — not generic tech. Look for accounts posting about:

- **Claude Code / Cursor / Codex / Windsurf** workflows, tips, "watch me build"
- **"vibe coding"**, AI-agent dev, MCP servers, indie hacking with AI
- **developer productivity / terminal / dotfiles** content

Micro-creators (5k–30k) convert better per dollar than big names and take flat
$15–30 deals. Avoid pure "AI news" accounts — their audience won't install a CLI.

## The creator brief (give them this)

**Product:** Kage — git-native, verified memory for coding agents. Free & open source.
**The one-liner they say:** *"If your AI coding agent keeps forgetting your repo / re-explaining the same stuff — this gives it memory that's actually shared with your team and checked against your code."*

**30–45 sec structure:**
1. **Hook (pain):** "Every new Cursor/Claude Code session forgets everything about my repo."
2. **Turn:** "So I added Kage — it remembers decisions, bug fixes, gotchas, *verified against the actual code* so it never acts on stale info, and it's shared with my whole team through git."
3. **Show:** run `npx -y @kage-core/kage-graph-mcp scan --project .` (60-sec Truth Report) OR the viewer (kage-core.com/viewer).
4. **CTA (always):** *"Free to try — one command in the description. Or book a 30-sec— I mean 30-min demo on your own repo at kage-core.com/demo."*

**On-screen / caption CTA (every video):**
> Try it: `npx -y @kage-core/kage-graph-mcp install` · Book a demo → kage-core.com/demo

**Do:** be honest, show it on a real repo, keep it casual/first-person.
**Don't:** call it "the best", bash competitors, or fake results. No "#ad" omission — disclose paid partnership.

## Negotiation playbook (the bargaining, scripted)

**Your numbers (decide before you message):**
- Target: **$20/video** flat, creator's account, you get usage rights to repost.
- Ceiling: **$40/video** for a 30k+ creator with proven dev audience. Walk above that.
- Perf bonus instead of higher flat: **$1 per verified signup** or **$200 per 100k views** — caps your risk, aligns incentives.
- Bundle: **3 videos for $50** (test cadence, better rate). Always ask for the bundle.
- Start with **5 creators**, one round, then only renew the 1–2 that drove clicks.

**Message sequence (copy-paste, fill brackets):**

1. **Opener** — see the DM template above.
2. **They reply "I charge $X" (X within budget):**
   > Works for me. To make it easy: $[X]/video, your account, 30–45s, your style — and I'd love rights to repost the clip on our channels. Can we do **3 videos for $[X*2.5]** so we can test what lands? I'll send the brief + a free demo on your repo so it's authentic.
3. **They quote high (X > ceiling):**
   > Appreciate it — a bit above what I've budgeted per creator this round. I can do **$[ceiling] flat + $[200] per 100k views**, which usually beats a flat fee if it performs. Open to that?
4. **They're hesitant / want to try first:**
   > Totally — try it free first, no obligation: `npx -y @kage-core/kage-graph-mcp scan --project .` or book a 30-min demo on your own repo (kage-core.com/demo). If you like it, we do the clip.
5. **Close:**
   > Great — sending: (1) the brief, (2) payment via [your method] on delivery, (3) the demo link. Post by [date]? I'll share the one-line CTA to pin in the caption.

**Always get:** disclosure (#ad), repost/usage rights, the caption CTA (`npx … install` + kage-core.com/demo), and a posting date.
